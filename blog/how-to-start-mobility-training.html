How to Start Mobility Training?

  People often overlook mobility training, but it’s a vital component of fitness that improves joint health, flexibility, and movement efficiency. Mobility training is not restricted to athletes, and there are several exercises you can do if you aim to improve your health and control over your body. Most importantly, it keeps you healthy and away from injuries.   In the following guide, you will learn the basics of how to start your mobility training. Read till the end and make your mobility training guide.  

Importance of Mobility Training

Mobility means the ability of your joints to move actively with complete control. Mobility training refers to exercises that improve this ability. Unlike passive stretching, mobility training aims to strengthen joint activity, as passive stretching focuses only on stretching the muscle, not its control.   Without proper mobility, your daily life will become hard. You might face health issues and even experience back pain. However, mobility training will ensure that you are healthy and you won’t have to suffer from stiff joints, which usually appear as you start aging.

How to Start Your Mobility Training? A Deep Dive

There are several ways to start your mobility training, and following them in order is beneficial, as it will form a proper journey in building a healthy life.

Check Your Current Mobility

Firstly, begin by monitoring your current mobility. Before you start, verify your limitations. The common area includes checking the mobility of your hips, shoulders, ankles, and spine. Begin with simple tests, such as the squat, shoulder, and ankle tests.    To help you visualize, squat and see how deep you can go. For your shoulders, know if you can raise your hands without arching your back, and verify if you can kneel past your toes comfortably.

Basic Drills

Start with basic drills, such as self-massage and foam rolling, for five to ten minutes. Then, try dynamic stretching for ten minutes. You can also try controller articulation rotations. Moreover, there are specific exercises that focus on mobility, such as the couch stretch, deep squat holds, scapular wall slides, and 90/90 hip stretches. For these particular exercises, it’s best to allocate at least minutes.   Additionally, consistency is key, so incorporate these mobility tips into your daily life. You can do it by doing a pre- and post-workout. Not to mention, include standalone sessions of 20-30 minutes at least three times a week. Remember, Rome was not built in a day, so don’t expect to see results quickly. You must do it gradually and regularly to see results.    Lastly, combine your mobility training with strength training exercises, such as overhead squats and deadlifts, for the best results.

Mistakes You Should Avoid

Most people make mistakes in mobility training that have a long-term effect, so you should try to avoid them. Firstly, never skip warm-ups, as cold muscles are much more prone to injury. Then, never overstretch, since mobility is about control, not flexibility. Moreover, never neglect your weak areas, such as the tight spots in your body. Lastly, inconsistency is a huge issue. In mobility training, regularly following it is the key to seeing results.  

Conclusion

To conclude, mobility is the key to live a healthy and comfortable life. Remember, it’s not only restricted to athletes. Starting it may prove to be hard but once you get the hang of it and start it consistently you will start seeing the results. Remember, your health matters and mobility is the foundation for your life.   The guide above will help you in starting your mobility training. As you progress, you can increase the time slots for the exercises and gradually witness the results. Also, have a vision and think about pragmatic results.
